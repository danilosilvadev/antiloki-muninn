the two-mail-paths rule, at purchase time

Cold mail: Smartlead, secondary domains only. Consented mail (invites, digest): Resend, canonical only. The canonical domain never sends cold — that's why it needs no warmup and why a burned secondary never touches your name.

the four don'ts

Don't cold-send from the canonical domain (ever). Don't send cold before warmup finishes — the code will let you as soon as keys exist; the domains won't survive it. Don't enable the LinkedIn auto adapter (P1 — operator-owned, deliberately unbuilt). Don't post the waitlist to HN — post the incident and the paper; the profile does the linking.

how you'll know it's working

The weekly digest is the scoreboard: replies ≥ 5.5% of sends and cost-per-positive ≤ $25 are the targets it measures against. Under those, adjust the angle mix and the dossier steering (reject-reasons feed the prompts) — not the volume. The 30/day cap is the conversation ceiling, not the infra ceiling, by design.
