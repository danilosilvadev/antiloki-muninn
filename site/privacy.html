Privacy — what we keep, why, and how to make us forget.

last updated 2026-07-16 · applies to this site, the waitlist, and our outreach
The short version: the waitlist stores the email you give us, plus how you found us. Site analytics are aggregate. If we researched you as a prospective design partner, it was role-relevant professional data — and one email erases all of it, permanently. We sell nothing to nobody.

// who we are

// the waitlist

When you join, we store: your email, an optional name, your position number, a referral code (yours, and the one that brought you here if any), and the campaign/source parameters your visit carried (utm_*, referrer). We use this to run the waitlist: your position, invite waves, and occasional product notes — the "one note when it's your turn" kind, not a drip feed.

  • Every email we send includes a working one-click unsubscribe.
  • Unsubscribing writes you to a suppression list that every future send is checked against — it outranks everything else in the system.
  • We never sell, rent, or trade the list.

// site analytics

We use PostHog to count things like film completions, clicks, and signups so we know which of our messages works. This is product analytics, not ad-tech: no ad networks, no data brokers, no cross-site tracking pixels beyond PostHog's own.

// outreach research (design partners)

We look for early design partners the way we build software: deliberately and on the record. For a small number of people whose public professional profile fits (think: agency owners, engineering leads), we may collect role-relevant, professional information — public posts, company pages, and business contact data from licensed B2B providers — under a legitimate-interest basis, to write one relevant note instead of spam. Concretely:

  • Volume is deliberately low (this is a founder writing to a shortlist, not a cannon).
  • Every touch is recorded — if you ask "how did you get my email and what have you sent me?", the answer is a query, not a scramble.
  • Reply "no" once and the file closes: you go on the suppression list.
  • Cold messages go to work email only — WhatsApp/Telegram happen only if you opt in.

// your rights

Access, correction, objection, erasure — email __MUNINN_CONTACT_EMAIL__ and it's handled, normally within days, at most within 30. Erasure removes every row about you and keeps only a hashed tombstone on the suppression list so we can never re-ingest you — being forgotten includes staying forgotten.

// retention

  • Waitlist: kept while the waitlist runs; deleted or migrated to your account when you activate; erased on request anytime.
  • Outreach research: purged on a schedule for anyone who never engaged; raw enrichment payloads expire on a clock.
  • Suppression list: permanent, by design — it is the record that we must not contact you.

// storage & cookies

This site keeps a referral code in localStorage when you arrive through someone's share link (so their referral counts if you join). PostHog sets its own analytics cookies. The waitlist itself lives in a Postgres database (Supabase) with row-level security locked to deny-all — the only write path is the join endpoint you used.

If this page changes materially, the date up top changes with it, and anything already promised (suppression permanence, erasure) never gets weaker.
